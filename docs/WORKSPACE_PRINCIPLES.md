# Gunimi Workspace Principles

These principles apply to every Workspace type in Gunimi.

A Workspace is not a detail page. It is not a CRM record. It is not a dashboard.

A Workspace is the place where business work happens.

---

## The Three Questions

Every Workspace must answer three questions immediately.

1. **What is happening today?**
2. **Why does it matter?**
3. **What should I do next?**

If the user must search for any of these answers, the Workspace has failed.

---

## Information Order

Every Workspace must always present information in this order.

1. Today's Situation
2. Suggested Next Action
3. Business Summary
4. Business Story
5. Supporting Context
6. Historical Information

Never invert this hierarchy.

---

## Structure

Every Workspace has the same structure.

- **Header** — entity name, type badge, health signal, owner badge, primary actions
- **Metrics** — the four most important numbers for this entity type
- **Tabs** — exactly 4, named and ordered consistently

Tab structure:

| Tab | Content |
|-----|---------|
| Overview | Situation + Next Action + Business Summary |
| Story | The continuous business narrative |
| Work | Tasks, notes, pending action items |
| Context | Relationships, memory, historical context |

---

## Decision Layer

Every Workspace must have a Decision Layer.

The Decision Layer answers: **what should happen next?**

Rules:

- Show exactly one primary recommendation.
- Base it only on real, available data.
- Never fabricate a recommendation.
- If there is nothing actionable, show an honest calm state.
- Never expose AI as the source.

---

## AI Principles

Artificial Intelligence is infrastructure, not a product.

- No "AI" labels anywhere in a Workspace.
- No AI dashboards, AI sections, AI scores, or AI badges.
- No Sparkles icons on business data.
- AI prepares. Gunimi presents.
- The user should experience better decisions, not AI.

---

## Calm Software

A Workspace must never compete for the user's attention.

- Every visible element must serve today's work.
- No decorative analytics.
- No empty widgets.
- No artificial urgency.
- No notification overload.
- If there is nothing to show, show nothing.

---

## Business Language

Every message in a Workspace must use business language.

Not technical language.

Not software language.

Business owners think about customers, deals, revenue, and decisions.

They never think about records, modules, entities, or database tables.

---

## One Place

The Workspace is the destination.

Context comes to the Workspace.

The user should rarely need to navigate away to find information.

---

## Success Test

Open any Workspace.

Within five seconds the user must know:

1. What is happening.
2. Why it matters.
3. What to do next.

Without searching. Without clicking. Without thinking about where to look.

If this test fails, the Workspace is incomplete.
