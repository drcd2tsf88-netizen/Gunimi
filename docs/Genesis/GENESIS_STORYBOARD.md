# Purpose

Genesis is not watched.

Genesis is experienced.

Every scene has one purpose.

Every transition changes emotion.

Every second earns attention.

Duration:

60–90 seconds

---

# Scene 1

## The Noise

Duration:
0:00–0:10

### Emotion

Stress

### Visual

Black screen.

Silence.

One notification.

Then another.

Slack.

Email.

Calendar.

CRM.

Phone.

Messages.

Everything appears faster and faster.

The viewer never sees complete applications.

Only fragments.

The camera constantly jumps.

Nothing feels connected.

### Voice

> Business has never had more software.

> Yet work has never felt more fragmented.

# Transition Rules

Transitions never cut aggressively.

Every transition should feel inevitable.

The camera should glide.

Never teleport.

Every movement must reinforce understanding.

---

# Camera Language

No shaky camera.

No rapid zooms.

No fake cinematic effects.

The viewer should feel calm.

The interface should feel confident.

Every movement has purpose.

---

# Motion Principles

Motion explains.

Motion never decorates.

Every animation answers a question.

Nothing moves without meaning.

---

# Sound Principles

Sound follows emotion.

Not action.

Silence is part of the soundtrack.

Small interface sounds are subtle.

No exaggerated sci-fi effects.

---

# Success Criteria

The viewer should remember:

Not a feature.

Not a screen.

Not AI.

But one realization:

"My business has never actually been understood before."

Genesis ends.

The Living Demo begins.

The story becomes interactive.
