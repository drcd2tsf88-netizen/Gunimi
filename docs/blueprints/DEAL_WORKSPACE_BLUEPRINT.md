# Gunimi Blueprint — Deal Workspace v1.0

---

## Mission

Deal Workspace is the operational place where a business opportunity lives from its very first signal until its final outcome.

It is not a CRM record.

It is not a detail page.

It is the place where business moves forward.

The Workspace should prepare today's work before the user starts working.

---

## Vision

Traditional CRM systems store information.

Gunimi Workspace understands the business story.

Every email. Every meeting. Every call. Every document. Every decision. Every task. Every relationship.

Everything belongs to one continuous business story.

---

## Core Question

Every time the user opens a Deal Workspace, the product must immediately answer:

> **What is happening today, why does it matter, and what should I do next?**

Only after answering this question may the Workspace expose detailed information.

---

## The First Business Signal

A Deal does not begin when someone clicks Create Deal.

A Deal begins with the first business signal.

Possible signals:

- Email
- Phone call
- Meeting
- Contact form
- Referral
- Document
- Manual creation

That first signal becomes the first chapter of the Workspace.

Everything after that extends the story.

---

## First Five Seconds

Within five seconds the user must understand:

1. What changed today.
2. What requires attention.
3. What can wait.
4. What should happen next.

The user should never search for this information.

Gunimi prepares it.

---

## Workspace Identity

The user should never feel they opened another page.

They entered an operational environment.

A place where work happens.

Not where information is stored.

---

## Information Hierarchy

The Workspace always follows this order.

1. Today's Situation
2. Suggested Next Action
3. Business Summary
4. Business Story
5. Supporting Context
6. Historical Information

---

## Navigation

The Workspace becomes the destination.

The user should rarely need to jump between:

- Contacts
- Companies
- Tasks
- Emails
- Calendar
- Notes

Context comes to the Workspace.

The Workspace does not send the user searching for context.

---

## Tab Structure

Exactly 4 tabs. Always.

| Tab | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| Overview | Today's situation, key metrics, business summary |
| Story | The continuous narrative of the opportunity |
| Work | Tasks, notes, action items |
| Context | Relationships, memory, historical context |

---

## Story Instead Of Timeline

Gunimi never stores isolated activities.

It stores the story of the business opportunity.

```
Email received
    ↓
Phone call
    ↓
Qualification
    ↓
Meeting
    ↓
Proposal
    ↓
Negotiation
    ↓
Contract
    ↓
Won
```

The user understands how the opportunity evolved.

Not just what happened.

---

## Work

The Workspace exists to move work forward.

Examples:

- pending decisions
- waiting approvals
- unanswered customer replies
- meetings requiring preparation
- missing documents
- stalled negotiations

Every work item should naturally answer:

> **What should happen next?**

---

## AI Behaviour

Artificial Intelligence is invisible.

The Workspace never exposes AI as a destination.

No AI dashboard. No AI section. No AI score. No AI badge.

AI prepares.

Gunimi presents.

---

## Business Memory

Every Workspace continuously builds memory.

It remembers:

- relationships
- documents
- commitments
- discussions
- previous decisions
- customer behaviour
- historical context

The user should never need to remember these manually.

---

## Calm Software

The Workspace avoids unnecessary noise.

No decorative analytics.

No dashboard overload.

No meaningless KPIs.

No competing widgets.

Every visible element must help today's work.

---

## Success Criteria

A Workspace is successful if, after opening it, the user immediately knows:

1. What is happening.
2. Why it matters.
3. What to do next.

without searching.

---

## One Sentence Definition

> A Gunimi Workspace is not where information is stored. It is where business moves forward.
